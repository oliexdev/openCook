# Shopping list & pantry

One shared shopping list the whole household can tick off together — and a pantry so you only buy
what you're actually out of. Both work offline.

## The shopping list

The **Shopping** screen is a single list for the household. You can:

- **Add items** by typing, or by [scanning a barcode](recipes-and-cookbooks.md#scan-barcodes).
- **Tick items off** as you shop — everyone sees the ticks update after syncing.
- **Edit or remove** items.

When **everything is ticked off**, a clear *All bought* banner appears. Tapping it moves those bought
items into your [pantry](#the-pantry) and clears the list — so the things you just bought are now
counted as in stock.

Items added from your [meal plan](meal-planner.md) remember where they came from, so you can tell
which recipe needs them. Deleting a recipe also clears its still-unbought items from the list.

### Shopping together

In a household the list is **live and shared**. One person can add things at home while another
ticks them off at the shop; after each sync both phones agree. If two people change the same item,
openCook merges the changes automatically (see [Family & sync](family-and-sync.md)).

## The pantry

The **Pantry** is what you already have at home. Its job is to keep things you own **off** the
shopping list. When the meal planner works out what to buy for the week, it subtracts what's in your
pantry first — so you don't re-buy the rice that's already in the cupboard.

You manage the pantry by adding, editing and removing items (barcode scanning works here too) — and
it also keeps itself roughly in step with real life:

- **Shopping fills it.** Clearing the ticked-off items moves them into the pantry (you just bought
  them, so now you have them).
- **Cooking empties it.** Marking a dish **cooked** removes its perishable ingredients from the
  pantry again. Staples (below) are left alone.

It's a simple "do you have it or not?" list — openCook doesn't track exact quantities.

## Staples

Everyday basics — salt, pepper, sugar, oil, vinegar and the like — are treated as **staples**:
openCook assumes you always have them, so they're never added to the shopping list or planned for.
This staples list is built in and the same for everyone — it isn't editable. The part you control
is your **pantry** (your actual stock); staples are the fixed everyday basics on top of that.
